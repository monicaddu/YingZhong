02 Reading Responses

(ONE)

What are some challenges you face when giving a critique?

There are couple reasons for feeling challenging. The first challenge for me when giving someone a critique is the language barrier. Sometimes I feel like I could give the feedback fluently in my mother language but could not find the accurate descriptive words to express my opinion. So I am afraid not to pass the my true feeling of the work. The second challenge is that I worry about giving biased critique. For some projects, although we were giving the same design brief, people would research in diverse directions and come up very different products. When I feel knowing little about the context of the product, I am not confident in giving feedback. The third challenge is that I hope to provide constructive advice to the person instead of just giving praise and agreement. However, in many situation, I get stuck and could not give really valid comment in a short period of time. When I have to give a critique, I will try to avoid hurting the presenter's feeling by providing logical evidence to support my opinion.

 How do you handle a critique that you disagree with?

I might repeat the critique in my own words firstly and comfirm with the person to make sure that I did not misunderstand. Then I will try to know the reason why they think in certain way from their points of view because it could be culture difference or we have different experience. If I could not figure out myself, I may ask they to explain more. I should thank them for giving the critique and tell them that I disagree with their thought by listing my reasons in a respectful and humble manner. But I will still keep in mind their thoughts and take them into consideration when refining my project. For my end, I should remind myself to not take the critique personally although it's very challenging. In most cases, I think classmates and professors at OCADU are quite friendly and they give really contrstructive advice in polite way.

 Why do you think it’s important to give and receive critiques?

I think critique help the presenter to recieve multiple point of views which he/she might overlook when focusing on one specific side of the project. It's a valuable stage to get feedback and fix the flaws before showing the project to the public. And during the critique, the exchange of ideas might spark unexpected inspiration. From my own experience, many creative ideas are derived from group branstorming.
Giving citiques is also meaningful because it practice my critical thinking. Also when I critique other people's work, I could reflect on my own project at the same time and may come up new ideas. Another minor but important point is that only if I respect and give others constructive feedback, they will show same patience and efforts on mine. :)

(TWO)

Where do you stand with the two ideological camps: flat and skeuo?

Well, there’s no definite answer to this. I think it depends on the context to decide which is better. And I believe the world will be losing its diversity if either of them dominates. So I would support that the best approach is somewhere in between. For example, if the subject discussed is a Subway map, I consider flat is way more efficient than skeuo. For example, a thick blue line could represents the lake that across the city, which is clean and easy to perceive. However, a skeuo lake that shows bridges and boats may make the map look too complicated and increase time for passengers to navigate themselves. But for a reading app, I personally prefer skeuo than flat. When turning the pages, the sound of paper flipping help create an immersive reading experience. Besides, some reading apps even offer several paper texture for users to choose for different tastes. So I pick the side over the consideration of the using scene.

 What is a zoopraxiscope and how does it relate to web and interaction design? Find another example from filmmaking or another medium that has inspired digital design.

Zoopraxiscope is a projector invented by Muybridge which can display motion pictures. It is considered mother of web and interaction design because they both share the basic of creating animation by displaying continuous motion images or graphics. Moreover, as Frank Chimero said, they "both work on screens, and manage time, movement, and most importantly, change." One example I can think of from filmmaking is storyboard. Storyboard is a series of sketches that shows the sequences of the scenes planned to shoot. It helps to communicate the story and allows the director to have a preview of the film. Likewise, when designing a web or app, designers would sketch out the layout of each page and create a lofi prototype to view the flow of the screen before designing the graphics. It's very much like the the role of a storyboard for filmmaking but it has more "branches" than a definite ending.

 Do you agree with that screens want "flux"?

Yes, I do. In What Screen Wants by Frank Chimero, he said "I believe screens have grain: a certain way they’ve grown and matured that describes how they want to be treated." and "the grain of screens is something I call flux." What he mean by flux refers to the capacity of the screens and its ability and possibility for change. I agree with his statement in the end that we should support a web that embraces diversity and openness. Our current perception of internet restricted our imagination of web and gradually numb our true needs. We are stuck in the frame we created. Nowadays websites and apps are designed to make its users more loyal and spend longer screen time. The role of people using the web transformed from users to servants of technology. And that's not the only goal of the screens. There are a lot more to explore and create of the screens, for what we enjoy. So I believe that screens want "flux" as long as we are aware of the distortion and on the way for better change.

(THREE)

What do you think is Lialina's opinion of the word "user"?

Lialina thinks that the word "User" acknowledged our relationship with computers and it protected our rights as the "user". By reviewing Lialina ’s point of view, the priority is to understand the difference between users and people. Users are usually defined as a special group where Designers will formulate different interaction according to different behaviors and characteristics of users. For different users, Lialina emphasizes the rights and freedom of users in accepting and using these interactive services. Besides, Lialina believed that people should not regard computers as the focus of interaction, because it should leave blanks for users, and the role of users are switch from knowing nothing to "know how to open doors without knobs" within this transformation, thus, users should be in a dominant position in this process. As Lialina said in the article "User is a human being and needs to be taken into account in design and validation processes", she believes that the user is before the computer and should play an active role in interaction design, not passively receive instructions from the Internet.

 What is Lialina's ideal internet user?

Lialina's ideal Internet user should take the initiative when using a network or application. Users can control the program autonomously in order to achieve the purpose they want, instead of being informed of various limitations, which is what she calls "The General Purpose User". One of the qualifications is that she wants users to have a strong sense of participation and integration when using the Internet, which is so-called the user's autonomy. Regarding the autonomy of users, she explained that "They can also find a way to publish photos online without Flickr, tweet without Twitter, like without Facebook, make a black frame around pictures without Instagram...", indicating that users do not restrict by a specific framework to enable the Internet to fulfill all their requirements. So she believes in users' ability to take care themselves and to find the solution without a software or program designed for a specific task.

 From your own experiences interacting on the web—what is an example where there are "gaps that can be filled by users"?

The quote reminded me an app I recently discovered called Notion. It's an app that can achieve the effect of "gaps that can be filled by users". Although it cannot reach universal use, this app provides applications with components such as notes, tasks, databases, boards, wikis, calendars, and reminders, and users can create their own systems for knowledge management, project management, etc. Notion provides users with individual and private use, as well as opportunities for cooperative use. Notion can also enable users to switch operations on different platforms because it provides both a web platform and a mobile platform. I think Notion's page can be counted as "turing complete" because its main page can meet the needs of users to the greatest extent, and it provides various web plug-ins for users to use when browsing other web pages with the collection function of notion. The users of Notion are like what Lialina imagined, they take the initiative to use the computer as a tool for their desired goals.

(FOUR)

Why does Laurel Schwulst advocate for individual websites over social media? Do you agree with her?

Laurel Schwulst advocates for more individuals to build their own websites because she believes the web is now dominated by capitalists and private corporations. These well-known websites and social platforms put promoting themselves and creating profit ahead of the real needs of their users. Users must create and post content within specified rules and restrictions, which Laurel believes leads to a lack of diversity and prevents exposure for those with out-of-the-box ideas. I agree with her, and I think she shares a similar view with the "ideal internet user" expressed by Lialina in Touring Complete User. They both support the idea that users should take an active role in the human-computer relationship rather than being controlled by technology. The website needs to have more voices and more possibilities, and it should serve human beings to reach their respective needs. It should prioritize its users' goal. And I believe a diverse and inclusive network will encourage more great and never-before-seen works and ideas.

(FIVE)

In the opening paragraph of her post, Seu asks you, the reader to join her to 'defamiliarize our tools.' What do you think she means by that? And how do you think that action relates to her use of the word poetry?

What Seu wants to say is to forget the purpose of our original interaction with computers and the functionality of how we interact with them, including clicking and dragging. These devices have long been familiar to us as tools to achieve various interfaces and experiences, to the extent that we have lost sight of them in our daily lives. Seu wants us to abandon the idea that we are trying to achieve something literal and return to a focus on the essence of these elements and tools. She sees it as a potential to dig the conceptual level of technology. By perceiving it as a form of language and identifying the limitations of various actions, computer arts can deliver meaningful and reflective experience. Like poems which use simple elements to embed a thoughtful concept, computer language also has its poetic potential to create unfamiliar conceptual art practices with its interactive features.

 Seu outlines 6 categories within her taxonomy of tool poetics. Choose at least one of these categories and define it in your own words. Now find a website or online interface* not mentioned in her post that you feel operates within the chosen definition. Explain why.

Regeneration: I understand this category as using technology to improve a traditional concept or object that we are familiar with. With the flexibility of web or other medias to better represents the idea fluidly and effectively.
I found this cute website from hoverstat.es called Flefixx which is an visual language system that creates common line work graphics to build different narratives. I think it is categorized as “Regeneration” because the designer tried to reimagine the process of learning a new language by “finding and creating images in the common combination of letters that made up words” instead of “according to etymology”. When learning a new word, it used to be quite abstract. The Flefixx provides a new way to meet a new word by its visual representation, which I felt very welcoming and universal. Also the digital presentation gives a dynamic feature to it, adding a touch of fun and formability.
